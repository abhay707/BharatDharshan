About Amber Fort

Amber Fort — also called Amer Fort — is a majestic hilltop fortress overlooking Maota Lake, 11 kilometres from Jaipur. Built from pale yellow and pink sandstone and white marble, it blends Rajput and Mughal architectural traditions in a synthesis found nowhere else in India.

The fort complex covers four levels, each with its own courtyard. The Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience) features a double row of ornate columns. The Sheesh Mahal — the Palace of Mirrors — is lined with thousands of tiny convex mirror fragments set in plaster, so that a single candle flame creates the illusion of a sky full of stars.

Key Highlights

01

The Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors) contains over 1 million mirror fragments set in geometric patterns — lit by a single lamp, it simulates a starlit sky.

02

The Ganesh Pol gateway is considered one of the most ornate gateways in all of Rajput architecture, covered in frescoes, latticed screens, and painted tiles.

03

An underground passage connects Amber Fort to the larger Jaigarh Fort on the ridge above — used by the royal family for emergency escape during sieges.

04

The fort elephant ride from the base to the Suraj Pol gate follows the same route used by Maharaja Man Singh I in 1592.

05

Amber Fort was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2013 as part of the Hill Forts of Rajasthan group inscription.

Visitor Tips

General
Beat the Heat
Arrive before 9am. The fort faces east and gets very hot by midday. Carry water and wear light cotton.
Photography
Best Photography Spots
The Sheesh Mahal requires permission to photograph inside. The view from Ganesh Pol at golden hour is unmissable.
Transport
Getting There
Take bus 500 or hire an auto from Jaipur's Badi Chaupar. Elephant rides available from base (book in advance).
Timing
How Long to Spend
Allow 3–4 hours minimum. The fort is larger than it appears. Audio guides available at the entrance.
Clothing
What to Wear
Comfortable walking shoes essential — the fort has steep cobblestone ramps. Cover shoulders for the temple inside.
Food
Eating Nearby
Anokhi Cafe near the exit serves excellent Rajasthani thalis. Avoid food stalls inside the fort.
